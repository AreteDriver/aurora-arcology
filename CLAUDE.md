# Aurora Arcology — Claude Code instructions

> Project-specific instructions. Repo-level overrides global guidance.

## Project Overview

**Type**: Investigation-board framework for narrative universes
**Owner**: AreteDriver
**First instance**: EVE Online lore
**Spec**: `aurora-spec-v0.4.md` (Phase 0 execution: `aurora-phase0-execution-v1.1.md`)

Aurora is a curator-authored investigation board: nodes (entities), sources (citations), and connections (claims linking nodes) form a navigable graph of a narrative universe. Connections are first-class objects carrying claim text, confidence scores, and supporting / contested-by source references. Vocabulary tables for `node_types` and `relation_types` are editable at runtime — no hardcoded enums.

The data model is the load-bearing decision (spec §5).

### Data model (spec §5)

- **Nodes** — entities (characters, factions, ships, systems, events)
- **Sources** — citations (CCP news, chronicles, in-game lore, Ash timeline)
- **Connections** — first-class claim objects with `claim_text`, `confidence`, `curator`, `supporting_sources`, `contested-by`
- **Vocabulary tables** — `node_types` and `relation_types` editable at runtime

## Coding Standards

### Provenance is mandatory

Every claim originates from a source citation. When adding a node, populate `sources`. When adding a connection, populate either `supporting_sources` or `contested-by`. When in doubt, downgrade `confidence` and add the contested-by note rather than dropping the claim.

### Confidence calibration

- `>= 0.9` — direct CCP source quote / paraphrase
- `0.7 - 0.9` — creator's structural read backed by multiple CCP sources
- `0.5 - 0.7` — creator interpretation; defensible on stream
- `< 0.5` — speculation. Always carries a `contested-by` note. Filter out by default.

### L3 user-paste licensing

Aurora does not redistribute creator transcripts. Raw transcripts and captions stay under `data/raw/` (gitignored). Only curator-authored seed JSON (paraphrased claim text + source citation metadata) is committed.

### Append-only audit log

Every node, source, and connection insertion writes to `audit_log`. Do not bypass this — Tier 4 temporal-layer queries depend on it.

### Runtime validation

All curator UI ↔ DB boundary traffic passes through Zod schemas in `src/lib/types.ts`. New shapes require schema definitions, not raw object literals.

### Vocabulary, not enums

`node_types` and `relation_types` are runtime data, not TypeScript enums. Reference by string ID; let the curator extend at runtime.

---

## Anti-Patterns (Do NOT Do)

- Do NOT hardcode node types or relation types as TypeScript enums. They live in the vocabulary tables (`node_types`, `relation_types`). Reference by string ID; let the curator extend.
- Do NOT reproduce verbatim transcript chunks in seed files. Curator-paraphrased claim text only. Brief verbatim quotes (a sentence or two) are fine for confidence calibration.
- Do NOT skip the audit log when writing nodes, sources, or connections — Tier 4 queries depend on it.
- Do NOT mutate connections in place — supersede them with a new connection and let the audit log reflect the change.
- Do NOT commit anything under `data/raw/` — transcripts and captions are L3 user-paste and stay gitignored.
- Do NOT edit `db/migrations/` by hand — they are drizzle-kit generated.
- Do NOT bypass Zod validation at the curator UI ↔ DB boundary; raw object literals at that surface lead to schema drift.
- Do NOT promote a low-confidence speculation by suppressing the `contested-by` note; downgrade rather than launder.

## Domain Context

### Why this repo exists

Narrative universes (EVE Online lore, Marvel comics, Tolkien legendarium, Warhammer 40K) accumulate so much canon that even devoted fans lose track of what is established, what is speculation, and which claims are contested. The standard wiki format flattens everything into prose, loses provenance, and treats every claim with the same epistemic weight.

Aurora treats lore as a graph of claims with citations and confidence scores. A curator authors seed files (paraphrased from CCP sources), the pipeline extracts named entities, builds a manifest, and dumps a navigable lens. Disputed claims carry `contested-by` references; speculation is filtered out by default but recoverable for analysis.

### First instance — EVE Online

EVE has 20+ years of CCP-authored news, chronicles, in-game lore, and community-canonized creator content. Aurora's L3 sources include:
- CCP news (`pnpm news:scrape`)
- CCP chronicles (`pnpm chronicle:scrape`)
- Ash timeline (community-canonized timeline reference)
- Curator-paraphrased seeds (the canonical layer)

### IP discipline

The repo is public; the source corpus is not. Raw transcripts, scraped article bodies, and creator video captions stay under `data/raw/` (gitignored). Only metadata + paraphrased curator-authored claim text is committed. This is the only way the repo can be public without redistributing copyrighted material.

### Out of scope

- Full-text storage of CCP articles, transcripts, or creator content
- AI-generated speculation surfaced as canon (speculation requires `contested-by` and ships filtered-out by default)
- Multi-tenant curators — single-curator-per-instance is the v0.4 design
