## Events

### Zephyr Ship Distribution *(YC111)*

In YC111, Polymath Valus en Diabel authorized the Intaki Syndicate to distribute the Zephyr exploration ship to all registered capsuleers. Sleeper-ignored by design, it allowed capsuleers to map wormhole space while quietly funneling telemetry data back through the Syndicate — and onward to Ishukone. The Zephyr represents the original instance of the Syndicate operating as a data-collection conduit, a structural pattern later replicated inside Upwell.

**Sources:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI) · [Ante (Chronicle)](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/ante)

## Persons

### Dorne en Diabel

The original unifying leader of the Intaki Syndicate and Silphy's father. Albino and charismatic, he established the Syndicate's autonomous-station structure. He died in a freak accident before a clone transfer could be completed, creating the succession vacuum that Silphy would ultimately fill.

**Sources:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI) · [Silphy (Chronicle)](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/silphy)

### Garyn en Diabel

Silphy's older brother. He inherited Poitot station after Dorne's death but proved politically weak; Silphy maneuvered around him to seize control and he was subsequently sidelined.

**Sources:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI) · [Silphy (Chronicle)](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/silphy)

### Mr. Lacante

A tattooed contact — likely Thukker — who appears in Silphy's *Antagonists* chronicle. He coordinates the Zephyr telemetry-data trade with Ishukone, described as "the kind of token Ishukone won't be able to resist." He is the mechanism by which Syndicate intelligence is monetized via that pipeline.

**Sources:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI) · [Ante (Chronicle)](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/ante)

### Salvador Sarpati

Head of Serpentis Corporation and brother of Sentona Sarpati. He collaborated with Silphy en Diabel during her black-market consolidation, making the Sarpati siblings the structural bridge linking the Sisters of EVE, Serpentis, and the Syndicate.

**Sources:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI) · [Silphy (Chronicle)](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/silphy) · [The Sarpati Family](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/the-sarpati-family)

### Sentona Sarpati

Reverend Mother and CEO of the Sisters of EVE, and sister of Salvador Sarpati. Once Silphy en Diabel's closest ally, she became her bitter rival — mockingly referring to her as "Queen Silphy of the Syndicate." She is conspicuously absent from the YC128 news cycle.

### Silphy en Diabel

Intaki Syndicate Chair and governor of Poitot; daughter of Dorne en Diabel. She was expelled from the Sisters of EVE following her collaboration with Serpentis. She subsequently engineered the Pas station famine to seize Poitot and the Aukron coup to consolidate power across the Syndicate. Arrested YC128.05.01, her detention broke the Syndicate's leadership structure and gave the Caldari the pretext to annex.

## Organizations

### Intaki Syndicate

A loose autonomous federation in the Syndicate region, founded by Caldari-sympathetic Intaki exiles after the Caldari Rebellion. Each station operates autonomously and the organization is officially non-political. The Caldari conquest of YC128.05.05 ended its sovereignty, though its membership network continued to operate — with capital ships escaping into highsec — as a fugitive financial and smuggling structure.

**Sources:** [Silphy en Diabel Arrested in Relation to Jita-gate Incident — New Eden News](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLp1gEBqEw) · [The Scope — Intaki Syndicate Fleet Attempts Breakout](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lfqboHJUNpw)

### Serpentis Corporation

Salvador Sarpati's black-market and drug pipeline operation. Silphy en Diabel's collaborator prior to her expulsion from the Sisters of EVE.

**Sources:** [Serpentis corporation strikes back against Upwell Consortium (YC117)](http://community.eveonline.com/news/news-channels/world-news/serpentis-corporation-strikes-back-against-upwell-consortium/) · [The Sarpati Family](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/the-sarpati-family) · [Serpentis Corporation Strikes Back Against Upwell Consortium](https://universe.eveonline.com/new-eden-news/serpentis-corporation-strikes-back-against-upwell-consortium)

### Sisters of EVE

A religious humanitarian order focused on Eve Gate research, with Sentona Sarpati as CEO. It maintains two distinct branches: a public humanitarian face and a secret research operation. The organization has run a long-standing Drifter and wormhole research program, and released Sleeper research findings as part of that work.

## Factions

### Intaki

One of the founding Gallente bloodlines, peaceful and philosophically distinct, with a religious tradition — the Ida faith — centered on reincarnation. The Intaki people are distinct from the Intaki Syndicate, the Caldari-sympathetic exile group that retained the name in nullsec. A claim of a "lost Idama" discovered in the Khanid Kingdom emerged in YC120.
