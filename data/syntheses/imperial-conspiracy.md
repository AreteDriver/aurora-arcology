## Events

### Aidonis Award to Heideran VII *(YC104)*

In YC104, Emperor Heideran VII received the Aidonis peace prize — a controversial Federation honor for an Amarr ruler whose Empire still practiced slavery. The award sparked a cycle of murder and mayhem that exposed civilizational tensions between the Empire and the Federation. Heideran's tolerance of the Pax Amarria reformist position grew from this period.

### Publication of Pax Amarria *(YC105)*

Heideran VII published *Pax Amarria* in YC105 — a seminal work on imperial governance, religious orthodoxy, and the Reclaiming. It established the modern Amarr theological consensus and became the canonical reference text that Catiz I's later reorganization partially repudiated. The book is commonly cited in Imperial Court rhetoric and the Five Heirs succession debates.

**Sources:** [Heideran VII's much appraised book 'Pax Amarria' published (YC105)](https://www.eveonline.com/article/heideran-viis-much-appraised-book-pax-amarria-published-1)

### Assassination of Emperor Doriam II *(YC106)*

In YC106, an Imperial transport carrying Doriam II's retainers was destroyed — Doriam II killed in the strike. The assassination triggered the succession crisis that placed Court Chamberlain Dochuta Karsoth in a de facto regent position. Karsoth's seizure of the succession is the structural origin of the Sarum-claim subversion arc that continued into Jamyl I's reign.

### Kor-Azor Cathedral Incident *(YC106)*

Also in YC106, an incident at a cathedral caught up in the Tash-Murkon renovation debate implicated Aritcio Kor-Azor. It followed directly from the controversy Catiz Tash-Murkon's renovation plans had ignited and the escalation House Kador subsequently pushed. The incident is a marker event in the post-Doriam succession friction between the Five Heir houses.

### Minmatar Elders Invasion of Amarr Empire *(YC110)*

In YC110, a Minmatar Elder-led fleet invaded deep into Amarr space, striking into the Sarum Prime and Mekhios theaters. The invasion was a companion event to the Caldari push on Luminaire the same year — together they define the YC110 wars and triggered the Empyrean Age. The Elder Fleet's eventual retreat through the Kor-Azor region marked the end of the Republic's deep-strike capability, and Jamyl's superweapon deployment during the crisis cemented her reign.

**Sources:** [Minmatar Elders launch invasion of Amarr Empire (YC110)](https://community.eveonline.com/news/news-channels/eve-online-news/report-minmatar-thukker-armada-invades-ammatar-mandate/)

### Sarum Prime Superweapon Strike *(YC110)*

During the YC110 invasion, Empress Jamyl I deployed her then-secret superweapon at Sarum Prime, destroying one Elder Fleet strike group. The other two strike groups simultaneously rescued Starkmanir and defecting Nefantar from the Mandate. The superweapon's use established its existence as canon.

**Sources:** [Lore Tourism - The Redemption of the Ammatar](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQUXg9vhPmo)

### Battle of Floseswin IV *(YC117)*

In YC117, an Amarr–Minmatar capital-fleet engagement — dreadnought-class warfare — was fought above Floseswin IV. During the battle, Drifters intervened and assassinated Empress Jamyl I. Floseswin marks the moment the Drifter conflict moved from a background phenomenon to direct intervention in empire politics.

**Sources:** [BREAKING: Amarr Dreadnoughts Destroyed in Pitched Amarr-Minmatar Battle over Floseswin IV](https://universe.eveonline.com/new-eden-news/breaking-amarr-dreadnoughts-destroyed-in-pitched-amarr-minmatar-battle-over-floseswin-iv)

### Empress Jamyl Murdered by Drifters *(YC117)*

Empress Jamyl I was killed in the Drifter incursion at Floseswin. Her death triggered the Amarr championship that Khanid II lost and that Catiz I ultimately won.

## People

### Heideran VII *(pre-YC105)*

The long-reigning Amarr Emperor who preceded Doriam Kor-Azor. Heideran's death triggered the championship that produced Doriam — the succession that Karsoth later subverted. His persistence prevented the kind of generational turnover the other empires experienced, leaving the Amarr political system unusually rigid by the time the Empyrean Age destabilization arrived.

Made a bid for the Amarr throne during the YC105 succession trial. A notable failed challenger in the Heideran-to-Doriam succession cycle, lacking a House backing strong enough to convert the bid.

### Doriam Kor-Azor *(YC105–YC106)*

The brief reformist Emperor between Heideran VII's long conservatism and Jamyl I's superweapon-defined reign. His assassination in YC106 left Karsoth in operational control of the Empire while the Royal Heirs were unable to consolidate succession. The Karsoth interregnum that followed set up much of the structural Amarr–Republic instability that fed into the Empyrean Age.

### Archon Ardishapur

Orthodox Amarr royal heir. He replaced the liberal holder Arzad Hamri over the Starkmanir and his religious crackdown triggered a Starkmanir uprising in which he was killed.

Son of Archon Ardishapur. In retaliation for his father's death in the Starkmanir uprising, he ordered the orbital bombardment that annihilated Starkmanir Prime. The Ardishapur house remains a major Amarr political bloc.

### Karsoth

Provost Marshal who took de facto control of the Amarr Empire after Doriam II's assassination. His refusal to concede succession to the Royal Heirs is the proximate cause of much of the Empire's mid-modern instability and explains why Jamyl I's emergence was framed as restoration rather than coronation. The Karsoth interregnum is also the structural ancestor of the office-holder-versus-throne tension that any modern Amarr political beat references.
