# Imperial Court Conspiracy (Karsoth → Sarum → Catiz)

> The Amarr post-Doriam succession subversion: Doriam II's assassination, Court Chamberlain Karsoth's regency, the subverted Sarum claim, Jamyl I's resurrection-and-Servitor cycle, and Catiz I's modern reorganization that partially repudiated Pax Amarria.

*Source-grounded synthesis. Curator-authored claim text, deduplicated. Every citation traces to a primary source.*

## Events

### Empress Jamyl Murdered by Drifters

Empress Jamyl I murdered by Drifters mid-incursion. Triggered the Amarr championship that Khanid II lost (forcing him into sheal-sin) and that Catiz I won. Without Jamyl's death there is no Catiz, no current Amarr posture, no 'Mandate of Faith' SKIN flavor invoking her call.

### Aidonis Award to Heideran VII *(YC104)*

YC104 Aidonis (peace-prize) award to Heideran VII — controversial Federation honor for an Amarr Emperor whose Empire still practiced slavery. The award sparked a cycle of murder and mayhem that exposed Empire–Federation civilizational tensions. Heideran's tolerance of the Pax Amarria reformist position grew from this period.

### Publication of Pax Amarria *(YC105)*

Heideran VII's seminal philosophical book on imperial governance, religious orthodoxy, and the Reclaiming. Published YC105; established the modern Amarr theological consensus and was the canonical reference text Catiz I's reorganization later partially repudiated. Commonly cited in Imperial Court rhetoric and the Five Heirs succession debates.

### Sarum Prime Superweapon Strike *(YC110)*

Empress Jamyl I deployed her then-secret superweapon to destroy one Elder Fleet strike group at Sarum Prime. The other two strike groups simultaneously rescued Starkmanir and defecting Nefantar from the Mandate. Established Jamyl's superweapon as canon — a precedent that informs the Catalyst-era doomsday site rollout.

### Archon Ardishapur

Orthodox Amarr royal heir. Replaced the liberal Arzad Hamri as holder of Starkmanir. His religious crackdown triggered the Starkmanir uprising; he was killed in it, which provoked his son's bombardment of Starkmanir Prime.

Structurally important as the named successor whose corruption became the explicit reason House Kor-Azor lost political weight relative to Sarum and Tash-Murkon during Jamyl's rise. Modern Amarr royal politics still references the Kor-Azor diminishment as a cautionary precedent.

### Karsoth

The Karsoth interregnum is the modern Amarr's structural disaster — a Provost Marshal running the Empire's day-to-day while the throne sat vacant. His refusal to concede succession to the Royal Heirs is the proximate cause of much of the Empire's mid-modern instability and explains why Jamyl I's emergence was framed as restoration rather than coronation. Karsoth's tenure is also the structural ancestor of the kind of office-holder-vs-throne tension that any modern Amarr political beat references.

### Imperial Court

Amarr Empire's executive court. The Court Chamberlain office serves as regent during succession gaps; held by Dochuta Karsoth during the post-Doriam-II era and used to subvert the Sarum succession. ### House Kador

House Kador anchors the conservative end of Amarr royal politics — most committed to traditional slaver economics, most resistant to the kind of incremental reform Tash-Murkon and Kor-Azor periodically push. When the Empire needs an institutional voice for 'the old order is non-negotiable', Kador provides it. Strategically aligned with Ardishapur on most religious-political questions; structurally opposed to Tash-Murkon's pragmatic accommodation strategies.

### House Kor-Azor

House Kor-Azor's institutional identity is reformist: Doriam II's brief reign was the modern Empire's last significant attempt at reform-from-the-throne. The house's reputation took a long-term hit when Aritcio's involvement in the Karsoth-era corrupt Provost Marshal apparatus tarnished the lineage. Kor-Azor's reduced political weight relative to Sarum and Tash-Murkon during Jamyl's rise is the structural memory of that failure — and the cautionary precedent every future Amarr reformist program references.

### House Sarum

House Sarum's institutional identity is military sufficiency. Their territorial seat is centered on Sarum Prime (destroyed by Jamyl's superweapon during the Elder Fleet attack — an in-house event with massive symbolic weight). Sarum's combat-doctrine focus made it the natural counterweight to Karsoth's bureaucratic interregnum, and Jamyl's emergence from this house reset the Empire's institutional center of gravity for a generation.

### House Tash-Murkon

House Tash-Murkon's distinctness is structural — Udorian rather than Amarr bloodline means it operates inside the Amarr political system without quite being of it. The youngest house, the most progressive on slave reform, and the natural diplomatic interlocutor when Empire–Federation or Empire–Republic talks need a non-confrontational Amarr counterparty. Tash-Murkon's region is also the Empire's southern frontier with Khanid (defected) and Aridia (lawless), giving the house operational reasons to maintain pragmatic borders.

## Concepts

### Five Heirs (Amarr Royal Houses)

The doctrinal Amarr-Empire structure of five Royal Houses (Sarum, Tash-Murkon, Kor-Azor, Kador, Ardishapur), each providing a Heir candidate for the throne under the championship-and-succession rules. The Heideran VII → Doriam II → Karsoth-subverted-Sarum → Jamyl I → Catiz I succession ran through this structure. The Three-Layer Amarr concept (Empire / Houses / Provost-Marshal frontier) interlocks with the Five Heirs.

### Pax Amarria Doctrine

Heideran VII's theological-political consensus from his YC105 book Pax Amarria — a tolerant Reclaiming and reformist Empire posture. Established the Heideran-era Amarr Empire's relationship with the Federation and the Republic. Catiz I's territorial reorganization partially repudiated the doctrine; the Cult of Tetrimon opposed it on orthodox-purity grounds. Modern Amarr political debates reference the Pax Amarria-vs-orthodoxy axis.
