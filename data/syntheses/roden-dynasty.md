# Roden Dynasty (Federation industrial-political arc)

> The Roden Shipyards / Roden family arc — from YC105 industrial ascendancy through Jacus Roden's presidency to Soraya Roden's YC128 candidacy. Federation industrial-political continuity across three generations.

*Source-grounded synthesis. Curator-authored claim text, deduplicated. Every citation traces to a primary source.*

## Events

### Federation Day (annual) *(YC0+)*

Annual Gallente Federation founding holiday. Cultural anchor of Federation identity; presidential addresses traditionally fall during the Federation Day cycle. Structural complement to Caldari Liberation Day and Republic Liberation Day.

### Federation Seizure of Intaki *(2022)*

The YC124 Federation seizure of Intaki + two adjacent constellations. Triggered the Caldari–Intaki Syndicate constellation deal that the YC128 conquest now invalidates. Reading the Caldari conquest as 'restoring' anything requires placing this earlier seizure as the original wound.
**Cited in:** [Silphy - The Faction Leader You Never Hear About (recorded YC126.04.04)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWvC8dlYBjI)

### Gallente Presidential Election YC128 *(2026-04-17)*

Moreau's narrow win over Roden inverted the expected hawkish response to Caldari escalation. The Federation elected a reformer at the exact moment State aggression peaked, structurally weakening the Federation military response and giving Mountain/Ocean an opening they wouldn't have had under a Roden presidency.

## Persons

### Alix Moreau

New Gallente Federation President. Autonomist Party. Inaugurated under the Jita-gate cloud. Issued amnesty to Syndicate descendants during the conquest, positioning the Federation against Caldari rule. Hawkish Senate factions worry her military inexperience hands the State the upper hand.

### Celestis Aguard

Stargate Trailblazer figure who built the Kenninck–Egghelende stargate. After the Caldari conquest of Syndicate, his stargate is the only contiguous Gallente connection — without him, Federation space splits and the State has structural transit leverage.

### Soraya Roden

Election runner-up. Roden Shipyards heir; Quafe-sponsored; hawkish military posture. Would have been the predictable response to Caldari escalation — Moreau's election denied Lai Dai/Forest the Federation hawk they likely backed.

### Inner Zone Shipping

Federation logistics megacorp. Major shipping company — backbone of Federation trade and a structural pillar of the Federation industrial economy. The Federation analog of Ishukone Watch in the State or Carthum Conglomerate in the Empire — a logistics company that doubles as a polity-of-influence within the Federation Senate.

### Roden Shipyards

Federation shipbuilder. Granted extensive Federation construction contracts YC105 (with competitors crying foul); built the first privately owned Gallente Navy-spec vessel YC107. The Roden family's commercial success underwrote Soraya Roden's later YC128 presidential candidacy — making this corp the structural backstory for the YC128 capsuleer-influence-election cycle.

## Factions

### Gallente (bloodline)

Gallente humans — the Federation's founding bloodline. Liberal-democratic cultural foundation; integrated with Intaki, Mannar, and Jin-Mei sub-bloodlines into the Federation. The bloodline's cultural-Gallente integration policy was the trigger for the Caldari Rebellion. Federal Defense Union and Federation Navy operate under Gallente cultural assumptions.

### Intaki

The original Intaki bloodline — peaceful, philosophical, religiously distinct via the Ida faith's reincarnation cosmology. Founding members of the Federation, but always retained cultural autonomy that produced both the Caldari-sympathetic exile (the Syndicate) and the Lost-Adama religious arc (Eloa Enaro / Negui Erlon, YC120). The Federation's seizure of Intaki in YC124 — what triggered the Syndicate–Caldari constellation deal — was an act against this people, not against the Syndicate. Moreau's amnesty offer post-conquest is structurally an attempt to reach the Intaki proper, not the Syndicate fugitives.
