## Events

### Seyllin Disaster *(YC111)*

A catastrophic plasma-fire event at Seyllin I killed everyone on the planet. The Sisters of EVE issued a 'Wormhole Colony Lost' announcement in the aftermath. The Seyllin event triggered the Apocrypha-era wormhole opening, the discovery of Anoikis, the awakening of Sleeper civilization remnants, and ultimately the Drifter emergence cycle. Every wormhole-tech thread in the warpath board traces back to Seyllin.

**Cited in:** [Seyllin Disaster as wormholes emerge (YC 111)](https://community.eveonline.com/news/news-channels/eve-online-news/weather-satellite-glitch-causes-travel-disruption-1/) · [Minmatar Search For Thukker Caravan; Unable to Respond Significantly to Seyllin Disaster](https://universe.eveonline.com/new-eden-news/minmatar-search-for-thukker-caravan-unable-to-respond-significantly-to-seyllin-disaster) · [The Fourth Seyllin Conference](https://universe.eveonline.com/new-eden-news/the-fourth-seyllin-conference)

### Apocrypha-era Wormhole Opening *(YC111)*

The system-wide wormhole opening triggered by the Seyllin Disaster exposed Anoikis to capsuleer access for the first time and awoke Sleeper-civilization remnants. This event started the chain of Drifter exposure that culminates in the YC125 Drifter Crisis.

### Hilen Tukoss Defection *(YC113)*

The YC113 defection of Caldari scientist Hilen Tukoss was a pivotal moment in the Drifter / Sleeper / Construct research arc. Tukoss carried Anoikis-research expertise out of Caldari corporate confinement. He was confirmed dead in YC117.

### Battle of Floseswin IV *(YC117)*

A YC117 Amarr–Minmatar capital-fleet engagement above Floseswin IV — pitched dreadnought-class warfare — triggered the Drifter assassination of Empress Jamyl I. Drifters intervened directly during the battle to kill her, marking the moment when the Drifter conflict moved from background phenomenon to direct intervention in empire politics.

**Cited in:** [BREAKING: Amarr Dreadnoughts Destroyed in Pitched Amarr-Minmatar Battle over Floseswin IV](https://universe.eveonline.com/new-eden-news/breaking-amarr-dreadnoughts-destroyed-in-pitched-amarr-minmatar-battle-over-floseswin-iv)

### Triglavian Collective Revealed *(YC120)*

The YC120 first major public revelation of the Triglavian Collective as a coherent civilization rather than scattered abyssal anomalies. The Scope reported the canonical introduction — a two-year warning before the YC122 Triglavian Invasion that conquered Niarja and the 27 systems forming Pochven.

**Cited in:** [The Scope - The Triglavian Collective Revealed (YC 120)](https://convocation.notion.site/37de035eb05149139ef4a3d1de9a9dfd)

### Triglavian Invasion *(YC122)*

The YC122 Triglavian conquest of 27 systems via the abyssal-filament invasion mechanic formed Pochven, opened the Triglavian-Drifter conflict to capsuleer participation, and produced the Niarja trade-route severance that broke the Amarr-Caldari high-sec corridor. EDENCOM's formation as an inter-empire defensive coalition came directly from the Invasion response.

### Fall of Niarja *(YC122)*

The YC122 Triglavian conquest of Niarja severed the high-sec trade corridor between Amarr and Caldari space, collapsing what had been the most heavily trafficked sub-cluster route.

### YC123 Dread Assaults (Tanu / Junfaa)

Mercenary fleets used illegal high-sec-authorized cyno beacons to land dread assaults at Tanu and Junfaa, establishing the canonical existence of empire-circumventing cyno tech. The same technology returns five years later for the YC128 Syndicate breakout — the Tanu attack is the structural ancestor of the Catalyst-era doomsday rollout. Tanu II is the capital of the Ammatar Mandate.

### Drifter Crisis (Final Stages) *(YC125)*

Five factions pushed Drifters out of wormhole space and took possession of Drifter pillar formations. Without this crisis there is no occupier list, no destructive-testing summit, and no Jita-gate. It establishes the access list that is central to the Caldari accusation against Gallente.

**Cited in:** [New Eden News - Warnings Raised Over Drifter Wormhole Experiments (YC127-10-30)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hPbfRmzc1bI)

### Drifter Wormhole Summit *(YC126)*

At a summit held at Jita IV-4, the five wormhole occupiers — Amarr, Caldari, EDENCOM, Minmatar, and Triglavian — jointly committed to destructive testing of the Drifter hyperspace network. This summit is the direct ancestor of the Jita-gate weaponization.

### AMP Raises Drifter Experiment Warnings *(YC127-10-30)*

Rio Talifo and the Anoyus Mapping Project went public with concrete telemetry showing erratic hyperspace anomalies inside Drifter wormholes and the risk of spillover into known space. Akimaka Saraki dismissed AMP as a Gallente puppet. AMP's warnings were subsequently vindicated by the Jita-gate incident and the Catalyst-era prismatic sites.

### Jita Gate Incident *(YC128-04-21)*

Hyperspace fractures damaged all seven stargates simultaneously, killing thousands and turning Jita into a politically untenable hub for the Caldari State. The fractures matched those observed in fractured fields, with ongoing radiation remaining visible from nearby systems. The mechanism — manipulation of the Drifter Hyperspace Network data-transfer layer — is only accessible to the five wormhole occupiers, a technical fact that contradicts the Caldari accusation against the Gallente.

### Eve Gate Collapse *(~861 AD)*

The wormhole connecting New Eden to Earth collapsed, severing the colonies from humanity's origin and triggering civilizational collapse across the cluster. Every modern faction descends from a colony cut off by this event. Sisters of EVE research is fundamentally an attempt to understand and possibly reopen the gate. The Drifter hyperspace network — the same one weaponized for the Jita-gate attack — is structurally the heir to whatever made the original Eve Gate function.
