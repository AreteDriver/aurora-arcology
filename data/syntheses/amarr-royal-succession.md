## Events

### Amarr Reclaiming

The Amarr Empire's pre-history expansion cycle built the Empire through the subjugation of neighboring peoples, with the Minmatar conquest as its climactic chapter. The Khanid secession was its first major failure; the Battle of Vak'Atioth was its eventual ceiling. Every Amarr-driven storyline traces back to Reclaiming theology.

### Battle of Vak'Atioth

The Jove Empire annihilated an Amarr Reclaiming fleet and halted the Empire's expansion at the Jove border, establishing the modern Jove-Amarr cold peace and locking in the four-empire balance. Every later Amarr crisis — the Khanid secession, the Minmatar Rebellion, the Heideran reforms, the Catiz reorganization — operates inside the Vak'Atioth-defined frontier.

### Mass Driver Strike on Starkmanir Prime

The Ardishapur-ordered orbital bombardment via mass driver annihilated Starkmanir Prime, constituting an effective genocide of the Starkmanir tribe. This is the structural origin of the lost-tribe narrative that the YC110 discovery later reversed.

### Aidonis Award to Heideran VII *(YC104)*

In YC104 Heideran VII received the Aidonis peace-prize — a controversial Federation honor for an Amarr Emperor whose Empire still practiced slavery. The award sparked a cycle of murder and mayhem that exposed civilizational tensions between the Empire and the Federation. Heideran's tolerance of the Pax Amarria reformist position grew from this period.

### Publication of Pax Amarria *(YC105)*

Heideran VII published his seminal book on imperial governance, religious orthodoxy, and the Reclaiming in YC105. It established the modern Amarr theological consensus and became the canonical reference text that Catiz I's later reorganization partially repudiated. The Pax Amarria is commonly cited in Imperial Court rhetoric and the Five Heirs succession debates.

### Assassination of Emperor Doriam II *(YC106)*

In YC106 an Imperial transport carrying Doriam II's retainers was destroyed, killing Doriam II in the strike. This triggered the succession crisis that produced Court Chamberlain Dochuta Karsoth's regency. Karsoth's seizure of the regency is the structural origin of the Sarum-claim subversion arc that continued into Jamyl I's reign.

### Kor-Azor Cathedral Incident *(YC106)*

In YC106 an incident at a cathedral during the Tash-Murkon renovation debate implicated Aritcio Kor-Azor. The incident followed the Catiz Tash-Murkon controversy over renovation plans and the Kador escalation, and serves as a marker event in the post-Doriam succession friction between the Five Heir houses.

Empress Jamyl I deployed her then-secret superweapon to destroy one Elder Fleet strike group at Sarum Prime. The other two strike groups simultaneously rescued Starkmanir and defecting Nefantar from the Mandate.

### Empress Jamyl Murdered by Drifters

Empress Jamyl I was murdered by Drifters during an incursion, triggering the Amarr championship that Khanid II lost — forcing him into sheal-sin — and that Catiz I won.

### Battle of Floseswin IV *(YC117)*

A pitched dreadnought-class engagement between Amarr and Minmatar forces above Floseswin IV triggered the Drifter assassination of Empress Jamyl I, as Drifters intervened directly during the battle to kill her. Floseswin marks the moment when the Drifter conflict moved from a background phenomenon to direct intervention in empire politics.

### Khanid II Forced to Sheal-Sin

After failing the Amarr championship following Jamyl I's death, Khanid II — who had previously avoided sheal-sin by defecting to found the Khanid Kingdom — was finally forced to perform the ritual. This closes the centuries-long Khanid avoidance arc and structurally subordinates the Khanid Kingdom to Catiz's authority.

## Persons

### Arzad Hamri

Liberal Amarr holder of Starkmanir who tolerated the tribe's heretical interpretation of the Amarr faith. He was executed by orthodox authorities and replaced by Archon Ardishapur — his removal is the proximate cause of the Starkmanir genocide.

**Cited in:** [Lore Tourism - The Redemption of the Ammatar](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQUXg9vhPmo)

### Archon Ardishapur

Orthodox Amarr royal heir who replaced Arzad Hamri as holder of Starkmanir. His religious crackdown triggered the Starkmanir uprising; he was killed in it, which provoked his son's bombardment of Starkmanir Prime.

**Cited in:** [Lore Tourism - The Redemption of the Ammatar](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQUXg9vhPmo)

### Idonis Ardishapur

Son of Archon Ardishapur. In retaliation for his father's death, he ordered the orbital bombardment that annihilated Starkmanir Prime. The Ardishapur house remains a major Amarr political bloc.

### Emperor Damyas III

The Amarr emperor who bestowed the 'Ammatar' honorific on the Nefantar tribe in recognition of their loyalty during the Minmatar enslavement era — the naming event from which the Mandate takes its name, and the act that frames the Nefantar as both traitor and trusted vassal.
